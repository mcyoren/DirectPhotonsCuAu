\begin{frame}{Basic Analysis Cuts}
   \small
   The nominal cut definitions are taken from the analysis note.  The main selections are:
   \begin{itemize}
      \item Event selection: BBCLL1($>1$ tubes)\_narrowvtx trigger and $|z_{\rm vtx}|<10$ cm.
      \item Charged tracks: quality $=31|51|63$, $|zed|<75$ cm, and $p_T>0.3$ GeV/$c$.
      \item Electron ID: RICH requirement $n_0\geq 2$, EMCal matching $|emcdz|<20$ cm and $|emcd\varphi|<0.05$ rad, and $E/p>0.6$ requirement.
      \item EMCal photon candidates: cluster $E_{\rm core}$ threshold, shower-shape requirement $\chi^2<3$, EMCal dead maps, and an electron/positron veto.
      \item Conversion pairs: external conversion-pair cuts based on variables such as $dzed<4$ cm, $1<r_{\rm conv}<29$ cm.
   \end{itemize}
   \vspace{0.4em}
   The systematic study repeats the extraction with controlled variations of these selections, including conversion-pair cuts, electron ID, electron-track momentum threshold, photon threshold, shower shape, energy scale, and $\pi^0$ peak extraction.
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Outline of the analysis method}
   \small
   The direct photon double ratio is measured as
   \begin{equation*}
      R_\gamma = \frac{\gamma^{\rm inclusive}}{\gamma^{\rm decay}}
      \approx
      \frac{
      \left\langle\varepsilon_\gamma f\right\rangle
      \left(N_{\rm incl}/N_{\rm tag}\right)_{\rm data}
      }{
      \left(\gamma^{h}/\gamma^{\pi^0}\right)_{\rm MC}
      } .
   \end{equation*}
   \begin{itemize}
      \item $N_{\rm incl}$ is the inclusive conversion-photon yield from the $M_{e^+e^-}$ distribution.
      \item $N_{\rm tag}$ is obtained by combining a conversion photon with an EMCal photon and extracting the $\pi^0$ peak in $M_{e^+e^-\gamma}$.
      \item $\left\langle\varepsilon_\gamma f\right\rangle$ is the probability to tag the partner photon from a $\pi^0$ decay, evaluated with simulation and occupancy correction.
      \item $\gamma^h/\gamma^{\pi^0}$ is the decay-photon cocktail correction from HELIOS.
   \end{itemize}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Extraction of $N_{\rm incl}$ and $N_{\rm tag}$}
   \small
   \begin{columns}[T,onlytextwidth]
      \column{0.50\textwidth}
      \textbf{Inclusive conversion yield}
      \begin{itemize}
         \item Reconstruct external conversions, $\gamma\rightarrow e^+e^-$.
         \item Extract $N_{\rm incl}$ from the $M_{e^+e^-}$ distribution after background subtraction.
         \item The conversion mass shape is shifted because the tracks are reconstructed assuming the event vertex, while the conversion occurs in detector material at finite radius.
      \end{itemize}
      \column{0.50\textwidth}
      \textbf{Tagged $\pi^0$ yield}
      \begin{itemize}
         \item Combine the conversion photon with EMCal photon candidates.
         \item Extract the $\pi^0$ signal from the $M_{e^+e^-\gamma}$ distribution.
         \item Mixed-event background is normalized outside the $\pi^0$ peak and subtracted.
         \item Residual background is described by a polynomial under the $\pi^0$ peak.
      \end{itemize}
   \end{columns}
   \vspace{0.4em}
   The nominal $\pi^0$ extraction uses a Gaussian signal plus a second-order polynomial residual background.  Variations of the integration window, fit range, and polynomial order are included in the systematic uncertainty.
\end{frame}

   \begin{frame}{Systematic uncertainties}
   \small
   Systematic uncertainties are evaluated by repeating the full extraction for physically motivated cut and fit variations.  Variations are grouped by source:
   \begin{itemize}
      \item conversion-pair selection;
      \item electron identification;
      \item electron-track momentum threshold;
      \item EMCal photon $E_{\rm core}$ threshold;
      \item EMCal shower-shape requirement;
      \item photon energy scale;
      \item $\pi^0$ peak extraction.
   \end{itemize}
   \vspace{0.4em}
   The systematic uncertainty is evaluated group-by-group from the spread of the variation relative to the nominal result.  The final band uses asymmetric up/down uncertainties with a baseline lower bound to avoid underestimates from statistical fluctuations in individual variation points.
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Analysis Issues}
   \textbf{What were the main issues of the analysis?} \break
   \begin{itemize}
      \item[$\bullet$] The earlier $R_\gamma$ result was too flat as a function of $p_T$
      \item[$\bullet$] At high $p_T$, the simulated $\pi^0$ peak was too narrow because of the simulation vertex treatment, which caused a loss of tagging efficiency
      \item[$\bullet$] At low $p_T$, the ${n_{cluster} == 0}$ rejection removed inclusive conversion photons without a corresponding tagged photon contribution
      \item[$\bullet$] This made $\langle\varepsilon_\gamma f\rangle = N_{\rm tag}/N_{\rm incl}$ too large at low $p_T$
   \end{itemize}

   \textbf{What concerns were raised in PWG meetings?}
   \begin{itemize}
      %\item[$\bullet$] Stability of the tagging efficiency correction
      \item[$\bullet$] Shape of $R_\gamma$ compared to the Au$+$Au reference
      \item[$\bullet$] Treatment of systematic uncertainties
   \end{itemize}

   \textbf{What was the resolution for these issues?}
   \begin{itemize}
      \item[$\bullet$] Corrected the simulation vertex treatment, improving the high-$p_T$ $\pi^0$ peak and tagging efficiency
      \item[$\bullet$] Removed the ${n_{cluster} == 0}$ rejection to avoid losing inclusive photons at low $p_T$
      \item[$\bullet$] Re-evaluated $N_{\rm incl}$, $N_{\rm tag}$, $\langle\varepsilon_\gamma f\rangle$, $R_\gamma$, and the direct photon spectrum
      \item[$\bullet$] Estimated systematics by varying conversion cuts, electron ID, photon cuts, energy scale, and $\pi^0$ extraction settings
   \end{itemize}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Physics message}
   \normalsize
   \begin{itemize}
      \item The measured Cu$+$Au minimum-bias $R_\gamma$ is consistent with the PHENIX Au$+$Au reference measurements at similar charged-particle multiplicity.
      \item The extracted direct photon invariant yield agrees with the Au$+$Au 40--50\% result within uncertainties.
      \item The Cu$+$Au integrated direct photon yield follows the global scaling of $dN^{\rm dir}_{\gamma}/dy$ with $dN_{\rm ch}/d\eta$ observed across collision systems and beam energies.
      \item The result extends the PHENIX external-conversion direct photon program to the asymmetric Cu$+$Au system.
   \end{itemize}
\end{frame}
